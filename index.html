A bit about myself

Hey, my name is Andy. I'm currently a student at Carleton University Studying finishing my last year at Carleton. I am Currently Looking for intership/start-up/job oppertunities.

I initially started my CS journey in highschool when I chose computer science in a latch ditch effort to find a somewhat meaningful and creative career for the future. I did pretty well in university throughout my years at Carleton maintaining a pretty high GPA. Later in my University carreer I started to become more involved in extra-curricular activities of my degree and started showing up to hackathons and meetups. I got my first programming job in my second year of university at Global Affairs Canada. The experience was memorable to say the least. I didn't like the pace and the culture of the environment and wanted to opt for something more fast and innovative I felt my time there was wasted in a sense as I didn't feel any personal or carreer growth and was working a 9-5 for the sake of working a 9-5. Later that year I received an offer from a larger organization to work for them for an internship but declined it to join my friend on his start-up venture. The reason for the choice at the time was that I'm still pretty young and working for a startup with litterally only 2 people would be a great learning oppertunity. The choice paid off as I got to see the in's and out's of a small company. I was able to make product key product descisions, move fast deploy features quickly, created database migrations, and a whole array of technical stuff an intern would not usually have the oppertunity to do. I then joined Scotiabank in Toronto for the experience of personal and career growth. I had a chance to see the booming tech industry in Toronto and how it overshadowed Ottawa in a sense. Scotiabank offered me the oppertunity to still work on project in a startup manor and I was able to receive mentorship from veterans of the industry and be able to have their input on a project.

Now I'm looking for my next oppertunity wether it be a small start-up or a well established organisation. I'm always up for a challenge
